The Apple Review

Written by Samuel Martin

Logline: A product review showcasing 5 different varieties of apples.


Scene 1

Action:

The camera starts from a birds eye view over the 5 apples.

The camera will then start tilting until it is level and facing the 5 apples

The second part will be where the camera pans past all of the apples showing one at a time. It will do this from both sides.

The final part will be a zoom focusing on one apple at a time and repeating this for all 5 of them.


Music:

Music Track - 'Blue Skies' - Youtube Audio Library

There will be no other audio in this scene as this scene is about showcasing the apples.


Scene 2

Action:

Starts from an extreme close up of the apple sticker showing which apple it is.

A shot showing all of the apples will be next.

For this I will pick up one of the apples and move it off the screen going to a close up where I will take a bite out of the apple.

This is repeated for each (5) apple.


Music:

Music Track - 'Claudio the Worm' - Youtube Audio Library

There will be no other audio in this scene as this scene is about showcasing the apples.


Scene 3

Action:

Will startout with a sideways pan showing all of the apples.

Then I will show all of the apples from a mid-medium shot.

Then I will zoom in on the particular apple that I will be talking about.

I will also show the apples from different angles during this time.

Will end with a shot of all of the apples and zooming out from them. Will also fade to black during this time.


Dialouge

Here is my review and thoughts about these 5 apples. Starting from...

The NZ Roze apple, was alright with a slight crunch to it and a rather bland taste.

The Simply Red apple was the most enjoyable one, with a slightly sour impact and a nice satisfying crunch.

The Jazz apple was all around fine, it had a nice taste and a nice crunch.

The Royal Gala apple was quite soft and really had no nice taste at all.

The Granny Smith apple was solid, with a nice sour taste and a very pleasant crunch.

Also, Here is a little display that I found at Countdown. It shows the level of sweetness to tartness for each apple.

And that sums up my review for these 5 different apples. There would probably be some change in my rating had I chosen a different selection of apples.


Music:

Music Track - 'Mr. Turtle' - Youtube Audio Library

The music in this scene will be rather subtle as to not interfere with the spoken dialogue

When there is no dialogue, toward the end the music will increase in volume for the credits sequence
